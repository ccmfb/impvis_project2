Common Mistakes in qPCR and Corrective Steps

2. Multiple Choice Quiz

Q1

Cq is earlier than expected.


Potential Causes:

- Genomic DNA contamination of RNA
- Multiple products or multicopy gene
- High primer-dimer production (when using binding dye detection)
- Poor primer specificity
- Transcript naturally has high expression in samples of interest

Q2

This data contains variable technical replicates, which have a difference in Cq values of more than 0.5 cycles.


Potential Causes:

- There was pipetting error
- Insufficient mixing of the solutions
- Low expression of target transcript - this resulted in stochastic amplification
- The reaction is poorly optimised
- There is a high Cq and low concentrations of the template

Q3

There are unexpected data values.


Potential Causes:

- The plate has been inserted backwards
- There is a poor specificity of the primers
- There are inhibitors in the sample
- The samples are labelled incorrectly

Q4

There is no data in the wells selecteed for analysis.


Potential Causes:

- The wrong dye is selected for analysis
- The PCR failed
- There is no expression of the target transcripton
- The first-strand synthesis failed
- The wells were not selected for analysis

Q5

There is exponential amplification of the no template control.


Potential Causes:

- There is contamination from laboratory exposure to the same target sequence.
- There is contamination carried over from the manufactured reagent.

Q6

The plateau is found much lower than expected.


Potential Causes:

- There are limiting reagents.
- The reagents are degraded (dNTPs or master mix).
- Some of the probe dyes are less bright than the others.
- The reaction is inefficient.
- The probe concentration is incorrect.

Q7

The comparisons between these samples are irreproducible.


Potential Causes:

- The efficiency of the amplification is below 88% in one or both of the samples.
- There is degradation of RNA.
- The differences in efficiency are more than 5%.
- The dilutions are inaccurate.
- The concentrations measured are low.
